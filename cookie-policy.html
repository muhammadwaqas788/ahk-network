1What Are Cookies

Cookies are small text files placed on your device when you visit a website. They help the website remember information about your visit, such as your preferred settings, which can make your next visit easier and the site more useful to you.

2Analytics Cookies

We may use analytics cookies to understand how visitors interact with our website — such as which pages are viewed most often and how long visitors stay. This information helps us improve our content and overall website performance. Analytics data is generally collected in aggregate and does not personally identify you.

3Preference Cookies

Preference cookies allow our website to remember choices you make, such as language or display settings, so that we can provide a more personalized browsing experience on future visits.

4Third-Party Cookies

Some cookies on our website may be set by third-party services we use, such as embedded YouTube videos, social media widgets, or analytics providers. These third parties may use cookies in accordance with their own privacy and cookie policies, which we encourage you to review.

5Managing Cookies

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings, including blocking or deleting cookies. Please note that disabling certain cookies may affect the functionality and your experience of our website. You can typically find cookie management options in your browser's "Settings" or "Privacy" menu.
